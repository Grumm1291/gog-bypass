Sign-in Exploits (WIP)

  • GeoGebra (Microsoft / Office 365)
  • Open GeoGebra Classic and hit the 3 line button in the top right. Click Sign In, fullscreen the popup window, then choose Microsoft or Office 365.
  • Click Sign-in Options, Sign in with Github, and click the Github logo. Search “google” in the top right search bar, select See topic on Google’s Github page, and click www.google.com on the right. You will be redirected to a Google page with no GoGuardian. Search for any site you wish to view.
  • Drawbacks and risks:
  • Some sites are slightly buggy or just slower than usual.
  • You are limited to a single tab, and are unable to go back pages, meaning you will need to redo the process if you are unable to leave the site you are currently on.
  • This is not confirmed but is important to note, teachers may be able to see the entire screen, including the popup window. However, it is likely a bluff by the teachers.

  • Nearpod
  • Download the Nearpod app from the Play Store, create a Nearpod teacher account using fake information, and click Create Lesson.
  • Click Add Content & Activities, then Web Content under the tab, then enter any .com link (e.g: YouTube.com, discord.com, etc.) and click Save. Finally, click Save & Exit at the bottom right.
  • At the main teacher dashboard, hover over the lesson you just created and click the Student-Paced button in order to activate your lesson code. Copy this code and paste it into the Nearpod app from the Google Play Store. Join the session and the site will appear.
  • Drawbacks and risks:
  • While sites will be unblocked, the GoGuardian screen viewer is not disabled. Do not use this during active sessions.
  • This method is slow to use, but it is an effective backup method if others are patched.

Certificates

  • Chrome Certificates
  • Go to chrome://certificate-manager/, click the tab labeled Authorities, and find the certificate authority labeled org-Amazon.
  • Click the arrow to drop down all roots. On Amazon Root CA 1, Amazon Root CA 2, Amazon Root CA 3, and Amazon Root CA 4, click the triple dot button, Edit, and deselect any selected checkboxes.
  • Scroll down to the certificate authority labeled org-Starfield Technologies, Inc. Click the arrow, click the triple dot button and Edit on each menu item, then deselect every selected checkbox.
  • Restart the Chromebook. Check the Classlink Launchpad. If it gives you a message telling you the site is unsafe, don’t worry, it’s completely fine. Now GoGuardian is disabled, and nearly every site is viewable*. The GoGuardian screen viewer will also be disabled, and you will appear as Offline to the teacher.
  • If you wish to reenable GoGuardian, go back to the Certificate Manager, Authorities, and org-Amazon. Click on only Amazon Root CA 1 and check the first 2 boxes. GoGuardian is now reenabled.
  • Drawbacks and risks:
  • You will constantly appear offline to the teacher, which may raise suspicion. This method is not recommended for use during an active GoGuardian session. Sign-in exploits are much safer during active sessions.
  • *A very few sites will still be blocked or you will be unable to access them. For example, you will not be able to use Classlink or Merriam Webster, even if you select to proceed into the “unsafe” website. The cause is unknown, although there is a possible link between extensions that open sites, such as Classlink.
  • Possible risk: Administration may be able to see the Certificate Manager, potentially raising suspicion.

Proxy Websites

  • Proxy websites can be used to unblock any website, at the cost of FPS. Most proxy sites simply ask for a URL. Type the URL of any blocked site you wish to visit, and it will load the site.
  • Drawbacks and risks:
  • FPS can be much lower on a proxy website than normally. Certificates are generally a better alternative, but should not be used as much.
  • Proxy websites unblock sites, but they do not block the GoGuardian screen viewer. Do not use these during active sessions.
